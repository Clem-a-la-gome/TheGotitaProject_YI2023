The Gotita Project is a remote measurement system for water quality.

Gotita @Own Creation

Our solution is based on LoRa technologies and LoRaWAN Protocol.

With the LoRaWAN protocol (Long Range Wide Area Network) the communication between objects can reach up to 15km.

The Things Networks (TTN) is an open platform offering secure connectivity services for LoRaWAN communication.

Example of the LoRaWAN architecture @The Things Network

Our nodes are built on certified IoT boards

Each node is built on a Pycom board that can communicate in LoRa RF. These boards are MicroPython devices that can use open source libraries related to the sensors.

Pinboard of the LoPy board @Pycom

These Pycom devices have integrated on their system multiple Analog to Digital Converters (ADC). Their purpose is to convert Analog signals into Digital ones, so the device can understand what are the outputs (measurements) of the sensors.

The Gotita Project measures 5 diferent water quality variables.

  • pH: The pH probe is built on a glass-based membrane. This probe is widely used because it's cheap and it's design is simple. Even so, this probe needs to be calibrated frequently and the error is higher compared to other devices.
  • Electrolical Conductivity (EC): The EC probe is built on a 2 cell electrode. Measruements have low error and the probe is very cheap compared to other technologies.
  • Dissolved Oxygen (OD): The OD probe consist of a galvanic cell electrode. Eventhough this probe is not commonly used in industry, it offers low error and the probe still cheap compared to other technologies.
  • Turbidity: The turbidity is being measured with a tubidimeter. This turbidimeter is not very accurate but it's very cheap.
  • Temperature: The temperature is being measured with a thermistor. It's cheap, has a good response and the error is low.

Product design

This product has been designed in such a way that biofouling is avoided and calibration frequency is decreased. These two phenomena are closely related to the amount of time that the sensors are in contact with the water.

We have added two electric valves that are controlled by the microcontroller. The purpose of this is to control the water levels within the device. In this way, we prevent the sensors from being submerged in water for long periods of time.
